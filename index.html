About Me 关于我

I am an Economics and Data Science graduate from the University of Southern California (GPA: 3.91/4.0), advancing to the Master of Finance program at the University of Chicago beginning Fall 2025. My career focus is firmly rooted in asset management and sales & trading, where I aim to merge rigorous quantitative analysis with strategic market insights.

My internship at Invesco Fixed Income (Hong Kong) solidified my expertise in leveraging Python for sophisticated financial applications. I engineered an NLP model using Python's Natural Language Toolkit to score Federal Reserve speech hawkishness, developed Decision Tree models for credit opinion analysis, and automated geopolitical opportunity frameworks (e.g., US-China tensions impacting Mexico/India markets). Additionally, I utilized B Quant in Python for financial report parsing and pricing analytics, directly supporting investment pitches for six open-end funds.

Beyond industry experience, my academic research underscores my analytical rigor. At USC, I conducted data-driven studies on Ghana's cocoa pricing policy distortions under socialism, cross-referencing Indonesia's success, and authored a published analysis on China FOREX and US commercial real estate workouts at CITIC Bank. These projects honed my ability to transform complex datasets into actionable insights.

Proficient in Bloomberg, Aladdin, SQL, and Power BI, I thrive in environments demanding technical precision and market intuition. I am now seeking roles where I can apply my Python-driven modeling, research-backed analysis, and passion for markets to advance investment or trading strategies.
